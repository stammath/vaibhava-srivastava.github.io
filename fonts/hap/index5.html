keeping track
Here's few specialized tips of writing emails more effectively. The excerpts are taken from Adrian Wallwork's famous book "Email and Commercial Correspondence: A Guide to Professional English".


    attachment
  • - Attached are two documents. The first is the statement on pedestrain risk exposures that I would like you to read and hear your comments on. There is actually a 400 word limit and it is currently around 650, so if you could find any way to remove a few words that would be great. Also attached is another statement on risk exposures of holiday drinking.
  • - Attached is the spreadsheet. Can you please update the crash counts (upto 2014) on the attached file?
  • - Attached is the first draft of the CMF report to be presented at the meeting on December 10. It is 85 pages long, single line spacing.
  • - Attached is the first draft of the CMF report to be presented at the meeting on December 10. It is 85 pages long, single line spacing.
  • - Attached is the word document on the data wrangling procedure that I currently use to extract the data from access database.

    • - Attached is the first draft of the presentation – if you could just quickly look through it and give me your thoughts.

    - Please find attached the spreadsheet on pedestrain crashes in Louisiana. The spreadsheet contains:

  • Monthy fatal crashes in all parishes.
  • Data merging VBA.
  • Risk exposure modeling.

    •

    comments
  • - I am sending you a Word version of the draft, so that you can make the changes directly using Track Changes – please don’t use the Comments function in Word.
  • - Could you possibly spare a few minutes to go through the draft version of the report and send us your comments? Your cooperation is very much appreciated.
  • - Your presentation is pretty impressive, well done. Here are just a few comments which you are welcome to ignore.
  • - Hope you find these comments useful, and bear in mind that I’ve only focused on what changes I believe need making so I’m sorry if it comes across as being very critical.
  • - Please let me have your comments on my presentation slides, suggestions for improvements in places and anything you think might be useful.
  • - I have now had a chance to look at your report, it looks very good. I was really impressed with your report. The only comments I have to make are in the section "Conclusions".


    • right person
  • - I don’t know if you are the right person to ask but I need the information about the source file of the D3.js in the company website.
  • - If you are not the right contact, please could you let me know who I should contact.
  • - If there is someone who is more suitable for me to contact, maybe you could forward this email to them.
  • - I know that this is not your specific field but I was hoping you might be able to put me in contact with the right person.
    •

    urgent reply
  • - Sorry to ask again but any initial feedback on my intial draft. We are very anxious to hear any news.
  • - I am very much aware that you have a huge workload and I certainly don’t want to waste any of your valuable time but I am sure you will appreciate that I need to get your feedback on my initial draft by the end of this month.
  • - I was wondering whether you had had a chance to look at the email I sent you dated 10 January (see below).
  • - I know this is asking a lot, but I really need an answer by 15.00 London time tomorrow.
  • - I am rather concerned about the situation with the sales presentation, as I urgently need to start going through what you have done on it. If you still need time to work on it, then we need to find a solution together. In any case, I would be grateful if could you get back to me in the next 2–3 hours.
  • - I apologize for having to ask you this, but I am sure you can appreciate my situation. Thank you.

    •  wrong
  • - I think you must have sent me the wrong attachment.
  • - Could you send your fax number again as I think I must have the wrong number.
  • - By the way I found a mistake in one your personal blog, under ‘Research Projects’ I think it should say ‘edge line’ rather than ‘edg line’.
    •

    introduce u
  • - Just a quick introduction: my name is Subasish Das, final year PhD student in ULL. I was asked by my supervisor Dr. X X to contact with you. She mentioned that you are looking for Transportation Researcher in TTI urgently and I would like to introduce myself as a potential candidate. Attached is my updated resume. If you have any questions please let us know.
    •

    look forward/other questions
  • - I look forward to hearing from you.
  • - Let me know what other questions you have.
    •

    reply back
  • - I should be able to get the revisions back to you by the end of this month/within the next ten days.
  • - I am sorry but I won’t be able to start work on it until Monday/before next week at the earliest.
    •

    when response late
  • - Sorry but for some reason my system thought your email was spam.
  • - Sorry I was convinced I had already replied.
  • - I apologize for not getting back to you sooner.
  • - Please accept my apologies, I was convinced that I had replied to you. Your best bet to get info on this is to ask the Graduate School directly. Thanks and once again sorry for not getting back to you straight away.
  • - I am genuinely very sorry about the delay on this Robert, I will get the documents to you as soon as I possibly can.
    •

    reply a reminder
  • - I’m on it. I may be able to send you something by later today.
  • - Sorry, I was quite busy today so I did not get the chance to look in to it. I may not be able to get back to you before mid next week – apologies.
  • - Please accept my apologies. I have been inundated with work and had hoped to get back to you last week. Rest assured that I will do my utmost to reply with the information you requested by Friday at the latest.
    •

    writing to u
  • - I am writing to you about the big data mining project in ULL BigDataU, a research group in which I have been involved for the past few years.
  • - Sorry, I was quite busy today so I did not get the chance to look in to it. I may not be able to get back to you before mid next week – apologies.
  • - Please accept my apologies. I have been inundated with work and had hoped to get back to you last week. Rest assured that I will do my utmost to reply with the information you requested by Friday at the latest.
    •

    thank you
  • - I sincerely thank you for your time to answer the questions.
  • - Thanks X. I just needed to know for my records.
  • - Thank you so much for reaching out to me!


    • copying/cc
  • - I’ve copied X and Y to keep them informed as well.
  • - X (who I am copying on this email) will work with you to provide this.


    • over phone
  • - I tried calling you but my phone doesn’t want to dial for some reason.
  • - Thanks X. I just needed to know for my records.
    •

    available
  • - I am available either day, but please let me know ASAP to eliminate a conflict.
