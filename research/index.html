Research

I am a Transportation Systems Engineering PhD student. I am an R enthusiast. I use two platforms Rstudio and Ipython Notebook to conduct data analysis in traffic safety problems. I have been working as a key researcher of five state funded research projects under the supervision of Dr. Xiaoduan Sun. The Research Advisory Committee of the American Association of State Highway Transportation Officials has selected two of our research projects (Safety Improvement from Edge Lines on Rural Two-Lane Highways / A Comprehensive Study on Pavement Edge Line Implementation) as one of its High Value Research Projects for 2014. I also co-authored the final reports of both of the projects. My civil engineering masters thesis "Evaluating Safety Improvement from Edge Lines on Rural Two-lane Highways" was also based on this award wining project. In total, I co-authored four state funded traffic safety final reports. I have also partially contributed in other two research projects in writing chapters. I worked as a coordinator of Southern District ITE (SDITE) 2011 Annual Meeting in Lafayette, Louisiana. I am keenly interested in data science and statistical learning. Recently, I have almost completed the course works required for a Masters degree in Statistics. I have also achieved verified certifications in 55 Coursera courses.

Peer Reviewed Papers

  • Factor Association using Multiple Correspondence Analysis in Vehicle-pedestrian Crashes Subasish Das, Dr. Xiaoduan Sun
    This research uses Multiple Correspondence Analysis (MCA) for detecting and representing underlying structures in a categorical data set, to analyze eight years (2004-2011) of vehicle-pedestrian crashes in Louisiana. [Published in the Conference proceeding of 94th TRB Annual Meeting, Washington DC, Jan. 2015 ].

  • Zero-Inflated Models for Different Severity Types in Rural Two-Lane Crashes Subasish Das, Dr. Xiaoduan Sun
    This research aims to investigate the application of zero-inflated models for different severity types in rural two-lane highway crashes. The findings of this paper will help highway professionals improve the safety outcome of rural two-lane roadways. [Published in the Conference proceeding of 94th TRB Annual Meeting, Washington DC, Jan. 2015 ].

  • Investigating Safety Impact of Edge Line on Narrow Rural Two-Lane Highways by Empirical Bayes Method Dr. Xiaoduan Sun, Subasish Das, Dr. Zhongjie Zhang, Chales LeBouef, Wang Fan
    This FHWA research was funded by Louisiana Department of Transporation and Development (LaDOTD). The research conducts the safety assessment of edge lines with benefit-cost analysis. [Published in Transportation Research Record (TRR), Vol. 2433, pp. 121-128].

  • Imaged-based Discrete Element Modeling of Hot Mix Asphalt Mixtures Dr. Mohammad J Khattak, Dr. Ahmed Khattab, Hashim Rizvi, Subasish Das
    This paper presents the development of micro-mechanical discrete element model for hot mix asphalt (HMA) mixtures modified with carbon nanofibers using the advanced imaging techniques. [Published in Materials and Structures, May 2014].

  • Exploring Clusters of Contributing Factors for Single-Vehicle Fatal Crashes Through Multiple Correspondence Analysis Subasish Das, Dr. Xiaoduan Sun
    This research uses Multiple Correspondence Analysis (MCA), an exploratory data analysis method used for detecting and representing underlying structures in a categorical data set, to analyze eight years (2004-2011) of single-vehicle fatal crashes in Louisiana. [Published in the Conference proceeding of 93th TRB Annual Meeting, Washington DC, Jan. 2014 ].

  • Investigating the Pattern of Traffic Crashes Under Rainy Weather by Association Rules in Data Mining Subasish Das, Dr. Xiaoduan Sun
    This paper demonstrates how to apply this data mining methods to discover crash patterns during rainy weather with eight years of Louisiana crash data (2004-2011). [Published in the Conference proceeding of 93th TRB Annual Meeting, Washington DC, Jan. 2014 ].

  • Sentiment Analysis of the Riders of the Bike Sharing Programs Subasish Das, Anandi Dutta
    This paper presents the scope of using sentiment analysis approach in evaluating the impact of a bike sharing program in Washington DC. [Published in the Conference Proceedings of 55th Annual Transportation Research Forum, San Jose, California, Mar. 2014].

  • Association Rules Mining on the Ridership Data of a Bike Sharing Program Subasish Das, Anandi Dutta
    This paper presents the scope of using association rules data mining in evaluating the impact of a bike sharing program in Washington DC. [Published in the Conference Proceedings of 55th Annual Transportation Research Forum, San Jose, California, Mar. 2014].

  • Analyzing Crash-Prone Drivers in Multiple Crashes for Better Safety Educational and Enforcement Strategies Dr. Xiaoduan Sun,, Subasish Das, Yulong He
    This paper conducts research on crash prone driver's crash record for better safety enforcement. [Published in the conference Proceedings of TRB 93rd Annual Meeting, Washington DC, Jan. 2013].

  • Investigating the Safety Impact of Raised Pavement Markers on Freeways in Louisiana Subasish Das, Dr. Xiaoduan Sun
    This study was conducted to investigate RPM safety impact on freeway crashes with nine years of data. [Published in International Journal of Engineering Research and Innovation (IJERI), Vol. 5, No. 2, 2013].

  • Four-Lane to Five-Lane Urban Roadway Conversions for Safety Subasish Das, Dr. Xiaoduan Sun, Chalrles LeBouef, Wang Fan
    This article introduces a crash countermeasure successfully implemented on two different segments of undivided roadways in Louisiana. [Published in Journal of Transportation Safety and Security, Vol. 5, No. 2, 2013].

  • Level of service for parking facilities Dr. Yuhong Le, Dr. Xiaoduan Sun, Lizeng Du, Subasish Das
    This paper introduces a level of service (LOS) concept based on a comprehensive parking study in Beijing, China. [Published in the conference proceedings of 15th International IEEE Conference on Intelligent Transportation Systems, Anchorage, Alaska, Sep. 2012].
