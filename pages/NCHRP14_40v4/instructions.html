Criteria for a Modified Roadside Management Protocol

  1. Roadway Geometry
    • Safety clear zone width
      • ROW width beyond safety clear zone
        • Enough ROW width to eliminate at least 1 mow pass
      • Median width beyond safety clear zone
        • Enough ROW width to eliminate at least 1 mow pass
    • Sight distance assurance
      • Taller vegetation will not interfere
      • Roadside topography
        • Acceptable for all slope conditions
        • Recommended for slopes greater than 3:1 to minimize mower damage and provide safer work zone for maintenance personnel.
        • Proximity to drainage structures
          • Larger vegetation can be closer to roadway if sight distance is not an issue and does not create overhang danger
          • Sign, Signal, and Luminaire Supports, Utility Poles, and Similar Roadside Features
            • Not recommended in locations where presence of larger vegetation obstructs view to roadside appurtenances
            • Not recommended in locations where presence of overhead utilities may prohibit larger vegetation
            • Number of access points
              • Site distance issues for urban/suburban context may prohibit large vegetation within ROW
              • Pedestrian/bicycle usage
                • Site distance issues for urban/suburban context may prohibit large vegetation within ROW
                • Prevalence of illegal/unauthorized use of the ROW may prohibit larger that can provide shelter and present safety issues.
                • Safety issues
              • Roadway Context and Classification
                • Urban Interstate, Freeway or Expressway
                  • Site specific
                  • ROW width
                  • Presence of barrier
                  • Presence of drainage structures
                  • Adjacent land use
                  • Presence of access points, pedestrians, bicycles
                  • Prevalence of illegal/unauthorized use of the ROW may prohibit larger that can provide shelter and present safety issues.
                • Urban Principal Arterial
                  • Site specific
                  • ROW width
                  • Presence of barrier
                  • Presence of drainage structures
                  • Adjacent land use
                  • Presence of access points, pedestrians, bicycles
                  • Prevalence of illegal/unauthorized use of the ROW may prohibit larger that can provide shelter and present safety issues.
                • Rural Interstate, Freeway or Expressway
                  • ROW width
                  • Presence of barrier
                  • Presence of drainage structures
                  • Presence of roadside appurtenances
                  • Adjacent land use
                • Rural Principal Arterial
                  • ROW width
                  • Presence of barrier
                  • Presence of drainage structures
                  • Presence of roadside appurtenances
                  • Adjacent land use
                  • Presence of access points, pedestrians, bicycles
                • Rural Minor Arterial
                  • ROW width
                  • Presence of barrier
                  • Presence of drainage structures
                  • Presence of roadside appurtenances
                  • Adjacent land use
                  • Presence of access points, pedestrians, bicycles
                • Rural Major Collector
                  • ROW width
                  • Presence of barrier
                  • Presence of drainage structures
                  • Presence of roadside appurtenances
                  • Adjacent land use
                  • Presence of access points, pedestrians, bicycles
                • Rural Low-volume Roads
                  • ROW width
                  • Presence of barrier
                  • Presence of drainage structures
                  • Presence of roadside appurtenances
                  • Adjacent land use
                • Local agency resolution/cooperative agreement
                  • Ability to maintain naturalized area
                  • Personnel, equipment, etc.
                  • Adjacent property
                    • Aesthetic issues
                    • Clear view to property, i.e., commercial property
                    • Conflict with agricultural uses
                    • Land owner agreements
                  • Statewide or regional management plan
                    • Compliance with DOT plans
                  • Other ROW permissions, i.e., timber removal, hay harvesting, mowing, volunteer groups
                  • Public perception/outreach/stakeholder involvement
                  • Pollinator management
                    • Types of pollinators, i.e., bees, butterflies, bats
                    • Vegetation requirements
                    • Cooperative agreements with volunteer groups
                  • Wildlife habitat restoration
                    • Type of wildlife, i.e., pheasant, ducks
                    • Vegetation requirements
                    • Cooperative agreements with volunteer groups
                  • Winter/snow issues
                    • Usefulness as snow fence to manage drifting/blowing snow on roadside, interchanges and in large median areas
                    • Potential for interference with roadside use for snow storage
                    • Potential for interference with snow melt drainage
                    • Potential for winter shadow affect
                  • Headlight glare reduction values for use in medians
