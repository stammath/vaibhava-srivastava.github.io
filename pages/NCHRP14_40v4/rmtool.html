Selection Criteria For Managed Succession


Is there enough ROW width beyond safety clear zone to eliminate one full mow pass (minimum 15’)?
Is there enough median width beyond safety clear zone to eliminate one full mow pass (minimum 15’)?
Will taller vegetation create sight distance issues?
Does you site have mower damage on slope?
Does your site have drainage structures that may be impeded by proximity to larger vegetation?
Will larger/taller vegetation interfere with existing signs, signals, luminaire supports, overhead or underground utilities, and similar roadside features?
Will larger/taller vegetation create conflicts at access point such as sight distance issues (vehicles, bicycles, pedestrians)?
Will larger/taller vegetation (particularly urban/suburban) create a desired environment for illegal and unauthorized use of the ROW?
Does your site have issues with adjacent properties that may question allowing larger/taller vegetation (commercial, industrial, agricultural)?
Is managed succession in compliance with your state or regional management plan?
Are there cooperative agreements in place for other uses of the ROW such a hay and timber harvesting?
Is this corridor being managed for pollinator and/or wildlife restoration/preservation?
Will larger/taller vegetation be useful for managing drifting/blowing snow?
Will larger/taller vegetation interfere with snow and ice management?
Will larger/taller vegetation create a greater risk for wildfire within ROW and beyond?
Can larger/taller vegetation in median provide a barrier for headlight glare?
